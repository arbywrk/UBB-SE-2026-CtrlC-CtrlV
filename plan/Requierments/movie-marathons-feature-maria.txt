1. The Weekly "Sprint" Model
The feature is built around a time-bound, themed event that resets entirely every week to keep the experience fresh and competitive.
    • Weekly Reset: Every Monday at 12:00 AM, the previous week's marathons end, and a new set of themes is released.
    • Themed Curation: Each week features specific "Sprint" themes (e.g., "The Nolan Mind-Bender" or "80s Slasher Week").
    • Submission Window: Users have until Sunday night to "Log" their progress and verify their movie views.
2. "Proof of Passion" Verification
To ensure a fair leaderboard, every movie logged must be verified through a mandatory engagement check.
    • Trivia Trigger: Clicking "Log" for a movie immediately triggers a "Rapid-Fire Trivia Check".
    • Randomized Assessment: The system pulls three multiple-choice questions at random from a deep pool of movie-specific trivia.
    • Success Requirement: A user must answer 3 out of 3 questions correctly for the movie to count toward their marathon progress.
    • Integrity: This system prevents users from simply ticking boxes and ensures only actual viewers climb the ranks.
3. Tiered Progression & Unlocks
While there is no permanent "Genre Rank," users must still progress through a hierarchy within the active week to access more difficult content.
    • Tiered Challenges: Some marathons are designated as "Elite." These are locked until a prerequisite "Standard" marathon is completed within the same week.
    • Prerequisite Logic: For example, you cannot enter the "Interstellar Master-Class" until you have successfully verified the movies in the "Sci-Fi 101" marathon.
4. Marathon-Specific Leaderboards
The competition is strictly isolated to the individual marathon, meaning every user starts from zero each week.
    • Event-Only Scoreboards: Every marathon has its own live leaderboard; your rank is unique to that specific challenge and does not carry over to other events.
    • Primary Metric (Completion): Participants are ranked first by the total number of movies they have successfully verified within that specific marathon.
    • Secondary Metric (Accuracy): If completion counts are tied, the rank is determined by the user's accuracy in the trivia rounds.
    • Tertiary Metric (Speed): For users tied in both completion and accuracy, the winner is determined by who finished the entire marathon fastest.
